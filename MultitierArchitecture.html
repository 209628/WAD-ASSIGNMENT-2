MULTITIER ARCHITECTURE

Definition:

- Also known as n-tier architecture.

- It is a software design model that divides a web application into distinct layers (tiers) each with a specific role.

- Each tier operates independently and communicates with the other tiers through well defined interfaces.

- This separation makes applications easier to manage, scale and maintain as changes made to one tier do not directly affect the other tiers.

The Three Main Tiers:

  1. The presentation tier is the topmost layer and it is what the user sees and interacts with directly such as a website or a mobile application interface.
  2. The logic tier which is also known as the application tier or business logic tier is the middle layer that processes the users requests, applies business rules and communicates between the presentation tier and the data tier.
  3. The data tier is the bottom layer and it is responsible for storing, retrieving and managing data in a database.
